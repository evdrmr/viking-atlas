# Project Description: Viking Atlas

## Overview
**Viking Atlas** is an interactive, highly visual, educational web application that maps the rich history, folklore, and mythology of the Norse world. The project spans both historical events (such as raids, battles, and voyages of discovery) and mythological structures (the nine worlds of Yggdrasil, the genealogy of the Æsir and Vanir, and the influence of the Norns).

The core objective of the application is to **paint the big picture** of the Viking Age and Norse cosmology by seamlessly blending:
1. **Mythological Narrative:** Starting with Yggdrasil, tracing the creation of the nine worlds, explaining who rules where, and mapping the divine lineages (from Odin's children to Loki's monstrous offspring).
2. **Folklore & Legend:** The transition from gods and mythical ancestors (like the Ynglings) into the legendary sagas.
3. **Historical Reality:** Chronicling the actual Kings of the North (Norway, Sweden, Denmark), the unified kingdoms, expansion routes, and the key discoveries of Iceland and Greenland.

---

## Target Audience
*   **Historians and Enthusiasts:** Who want to explore logical and chronological transitions between legend and historical reality.
*   **Mythology Lovers:** Interested in an interactive, structural exploration of the Norse pantheon and cosmos.
*   **Students & Educators:** Seeking an engaging, interactive visual tool mapping medieval Scandinavian geography, battles, and migration.

---

## Core Pillars & Visual Concepts

### 1. Dualism of Myth & History
The application operates in two distinct states or "forks" that share the same visual interface but display different data overlays:
*   **Historical Mode:** Highlights real geographical locations (e.g., Trondheim/Nidaros, Lindisfarne, Dublin, L'Anse aux Meadows), historical battle sites (Svolder, Stiklestad, Stamford Bridge), and historical lineages.
*   **Mythological Mode:** Replaces the physical map with a runic overlay, rendering Yggdrasil's roots, the homes of the gods (Asgard, Jotunheim), and legendary events (like the death of Baldr).

### 2. Time & Narrative Continuity
A central "Story Controller" synchronizes three visual elements:
*   **The Map:** Shows where events occurred and draws routes.
*   **The Timeline:** Situates the event chronologically.
*   **The Dynastic Tree:** Shows who ruled and their ancestral connections.

As the user navigates through time, all three interfaces update in real-time, creating a cohesive, immersive learning experience.
